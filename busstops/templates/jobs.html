Jobs

Come and work on bustimes.org – the #3 UK ground transportation website, according to SimilarWeb. So far I’ve been a one-person company, but in 2024 it’s time to hire the first employee or two.

You should have:

  • some experience of web/software development. You don’t need to be familiar with all of our technology stack (Python, Django, TypeScript) or public transport data yet, but it might be relevant if you are
  • an interest in building fast, accessible websites or delightful apps
  • maybe some ideas for making bustimes.org better
  • the right to work in the UK

Depending on your particular specialisms, you might be working on (for example):

  • The bustimes.org API – it’s currently undocumented and incomplete, but could eventually power new apps, websites and hardware
  • User experience improvements – the website is too often described as “clunky”
  • Expanding coverage – more countries or modes of transport
  • Mobile apps?
  • Your own ideas
  • New projects
  • Location: remote (UK) or hybrid (Norwich?)
  • Full-time/part-time/freelance

    • Standard employee benefits:

  • Annual salary: £33,000–£55,000+ depending on experience (pro-rated if part-time)
  • 30 days holiday per year, plus bank holidays and leap days (pro-rated if part-time)
